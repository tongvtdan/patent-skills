# Licensing Frameworks & Royalty Reference

## Georgia-Pacific Factors (Full List)

Courts use these 15 factors to determine a "reasonable royalty" in US patent litigation. Licensing professionals use them for negotiation benchmarking.

1. **Royalties received by the patentee** for licensing the patent in suit
2. **Rates paid by the licensee for use of other patents** comparable to the patent in suit
3. **Nature and scope of the license** — exclusive/non-exclusive, geographic restrictions, field of use
4. **Licensor's established policy** — maintaining exclusivity, or general licensing policy
5. **Commercial relationship** between licensor and licensee — competitors or complementors?
6. **Effect of the invention on promoting sales** of other products of the licensee
7. **Duration of the patent** and the term of the license
8. **Established profitability of the product** made under the patent; commercial success; current popularity
9. **Utility and advantages** of the patent property over old modes or devices
10. **Nature of the patented invention**; character of commercial embodiment as owned and produced by licensor
11. **Extent to which the infringer has made use** of the invention and any evidence probative of the value of such use
12. **Portion of profit or selling price** customary in the particular business for use of the invention or analogous inventions
13. **Portion of realizable profit** that should be credited to the invention as distinguished from non-patented elements, manufacturing process, business risks, or significant features or improvements added by the infringer
14. **Opinion testimony of qualified experts**
15. **Hypothetical negotiation**: The amount that a licensor and licensee would have agreed upon at the time the infringement began, if both had been reasonably and voluntarily trying to reach an agreement

## Royalty Base Definitions

**Net Sales**: Gross sales revenue minus: returns, allowances, discounts, freight, insurance, duties, taxes

**Per Unit**: Fixed dollar amount per unit sold (avoids price erosion risk for licensor)

**Per Use**: For software or services — per API call, per transaction, per user

**Milestone-Based**: Upfront + milestone payments at development stages + royalty on commercialization

**Minimum Annual Royalty (MAR)**: Guaranteed floor regardless of sales — prevents licensee from shelving the technology

## Vietnamese Technology Transfer Law Summary

*Law No. 07/2017/QH14 (Technology Transfer Law 2017) + Decree 76/2018/NĐ-CP*

### What Must Be Registered
- Technology transfers involving state budget funding: **MANDATORY** registration with MOST
- Technology from state-owned enterprises to non-state entities: **MANDATORY**
- All other technology transfers: **VOLUNTARY** (but encouraged for tax benefits)

### Registration Process
1. Submit application to Ministry of Science and Technology (Bộ KH&CN) or provincial DOST
2. Documents: Transfer contract, technical description of technology, evidence of technology ownership
3. Processing time: 30 working days
4. Certificate: Technology Transfer Certificate (Giấy chứng nhận đăng ký chuyển giao công nghệ)

### Tax Treatment
| Item | Tax Rate | Conditions |
|------|---------|-----------|
| Royalties received by VN resident | Included in CIT income | Standard corporate rate |
| Royalties paid to foreign licensor | 10% withholding tax | Per Vietnamese-foreign tax treaties may reduce |
| Technology transfer income (exempt) | 0% CIT | If technology from state research, conditions apply |
| Hi-tech enterprise IP income | 10% for 30 years | Certified hi-tech enterprise in hi-tech zones |

### IP as Capital Contribution
Procedure under Civil Code 2015 + IP Law 2022:
1. IP valuation by certified valuator (Tổ chức định giá)
2. Board resolution approving IP contribution at valuation
3. IP assignment or license to company recorded
4. Registration at NOIP of new ownership/licensee
5. Business registration reflecting IP as charter capital

## License Agreement Key Clauses (Quick Reference)

**Licensor protections:**
- Right to audit licensee's sales records
- Most-favored-nation clause (if you give better terms to others, we get them too)
- Minimum royalty guarantees
- Right to terminate if licensee goes bankrupt
- Patent prosecution control remains with licensor

**Licensee protections:**
- Licensor representations of ownership and non-infringement
- Indemnification against third-party IP claims
- Grant-back clause (licensor gets license to improvements, but licensee keeps ownership)
- Right to sublicense (needed for sales channel partners)
- Termination cure period (not immediate termination for minor breach)
